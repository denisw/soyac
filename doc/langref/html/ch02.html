The structure of a Soya module () is defined by two context-free grammars. The lexical grammar defines how a sequence of characters is to be translated into a sequence of tokens. The syntactic grammar specifies how these tokens are then translated into a valid Soya module.

Both grammars are specified using the Extended BNF metalanguage (ISO/IEC 14977). The terms "production", "terminal symbol", and "non-terminal symbol" are used in this specification are defined in the EBNF standard.

2.1. Lexical Grammar

The lexical grammar of Soya is presented in (3). It's terminal symbols are the characters of the Unicode character set. The productions of the lexical grammar specify how these characters are translated into a sequence of input elements, consisting of tokens (), white space () and comments ().

Every Soya module must conform with the input production of the lexical grammar ().

2.2. Syntactic Grammar

The syntactical grammar of Soya is presented in chapters 4 through ?. It's terminal symbols are the tokens defined by the lexical grammar. The productions of the grammar specify how these tokens are translated to a valid Soya module.

Every Soya module must conform with the module production of the syntactic grammar ().
